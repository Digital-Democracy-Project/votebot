Digital Democracy Project (DDP)OverviewDigital Democracy Project (DDP) is a nonpartisan, nonprofit civic technology organization providing a voter-driven system of government for the 21st century. DDP enables verified U.S. voters to directly participate in the legislative process by voting on real legislation and tracking how elected representatives vote in comparison.Tagline: A voter-driven system of government for the 21st Century.Catch phrase: You vote. We track it. So you know the score.At its core, Digital Democracy Project exists to strengthen democratic accountability by closing the feedback loop between voters and legislatorsﾑbetween what voters want and what legislators deliver.What Digital Democracy Project IsDigital Democracy Project is a free civic engagement platform consisting of:* A mobile voting app that allows voters to cast ballots on the same bills as their elected representatives* An online accountability system that publicly reports aggregated voter results and compares them to legislative voting recordsDDP is:* Nonpartisan ﾑ it does not support or oppose political parties or candidates* Nonprofit ﾑ a registered 501(c)(3) public charity* Issue-focused ﾑ centered on legislation, not campaigns or demographicsDDP does not track political parties, ideology, demographics, or personal identity once voter eligibility is verified.Where Digital Democracy Project Is ActiveDigital Democracy Project currently supports:Federal Legislation* All U.S. voters nationwide may participate in voting on Congressional bills (U.S. House and Senate)State LegislationDDP is actively running statewide legislative voting for:* Florida* Virginia* Washington* Utah* Arizona* Michigan* MassachusettsExpansion Roadmap* DDP plans to support all 50 state legislatures by the 2027 legislative sessionHow Digital Democracy Project WorksDigital Democracy Project operates on a simple and transparent framework:1. VOTEVerified voters cast secure ballots on pending legislation using a mobile app.To support informed participation, voters have access to:* Plain-language, nonpartisan bill summaries* Links to official legislative sources and updates* Civil, structured discussion tools for reasoned debate2. TALLYDDP aggregates voter ballots in real time.Results are displayed publicly by:* Bill* Legislative district* State or federal chamberOnly aggregated results are shown. Individual votes are never disclosed.3. SCOREDDP compares what voters wanted with how legislators actually voted.This comparison produces:* Issue-by-issue vote comparisons* Cumulative Legislative Accountability Scorecards for each representativeThese scorecards allow voters to evaluate representation both during a legislative session and on Election Day.Voter Eligibility, Verification, and PrivacyDigital Democracy Project is designed for real, registered voters while preserving ballot secrecy.Key principles:* Voters are verified as registered voters in their state before voting* Multi-step verification and modern security practices are used to prevent fraud* Voter identity is never stored or shared after verification* Votes are recorded anonymously and reported only in aggregateDDP explicitly does not collect or analyze:* Party affiliation* Demographics* Ideology* Individual voting historiesThe system prioritizes both trust and privacy.How Legislators Use Digital Democracy ProjectLegislators can use DDP to:* Understand what their constituents want on specific bills* View real-time aggregated voter input by district* Use voter results to inform their legislative decisionsA common explanation from legislators to constituents:ﾒWith Digital Democracy, voters use an app on their phones to let me know what they want on actual legislation. I use that information to inform my votes.ﾓParticipation does not bind legislators but increases transparency and accountability.Why Digital Democracy Project ExistsTraditional elections provide voter input only once every few years. Digital Democracy Project extends democratic participation into the day-to-day work of governing.DDP changes the voterﾐlegislator relationship by:* Giving voters a direct voice on legislation as it happens* Making legislative accountability visible and measurable* Providing voters with clear, accessible information before electionsThe result is a more transparent, responsive, and accountable democratic system.Organizational Details* Legal status: Nonprofit, 501(c)(3)* Approach: Nonpartisan civic technology* Cost to voters: Free* Primary website: https://digitaldemocracyproject.orgCore principle: Accountability. Accountability. Accountability.SummaryDigital Democracy Project is a nationwide civic technology platform that allows verified voters to vote on real legislation, see what other voters want, and track how well elected officials represent their constituents.By combining mobile voting, real-time aggregation, and public accountability scorecards, DDP delivers a practical, scalable model for a voter-driven system of government in the 21st century.
